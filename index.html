About MySelf

I am a Provost Early Career Fellow and Postdoctoral Fellow at the Addition Research Institute of Steve Hicks School of Social Work at The University of Texas at Austin, United States.

I have obtained Ph.D in Computer Science from Instituto Politécnico Nacional, where I studied in Natural Language and Text Processing Laboratory in Center for Computing Research (Centro de Investigación en Computación).


My scholarly focus is centered on Natural Language Processing (NLP) for Social Good, Health Data Analytics, and the application of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning in the realm of addiction research. A steadfast objective guiding my professional journey is to immerse myself in research that bridges NLP and Health Informatics. My focus is honed on addressing complex societal issues with the help of machine learning, such as eradicating issues attached to substance use disorders, curtailing the spread of fake news, and leveraging health data through analytics and visualizations to fortify decision-making—all through the adept application of machine learning and NLP, with a particular emphasis on the development and rigorous evaluation of NLP algorithms using both medical and non-medical data to uplift society and improve public health systems.

In my spare time, I love to get in touch with nature because I reckon distances and differences are the secret tonics of creativity. I always try to get ready for the new experience and understand the beauty of nature. I like to develop social attraction with people and know about their valuable information. I am a very open minded person with positive attitude and love to meet open minded people too.
